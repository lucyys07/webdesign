Frequently Asked Questions
Q. How do I apply to colleges in Alabama?

A. To apply to any college in Alabama, start by visiting the college's official website and navigating to the admissions section. Here, you'll find application forms and instructions. Most colleges accept the Common Application or their specific online applications. Be sure to check the application deadlines and required documents such as high school transcripts, SAT/ACT scores, and recommendation letters.
Q. What financial aid options are available?

A. Students can apply for federal financial aid, state-specific aids, institutional aids, and private scholarships. Begin by filling out the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) to determine your eligibility for federal and state financial aid. Q. Do I qualify for in-state tuition?


A. In-state tuition rates are available to residents of Alabama. Typically, you must have lived in Alabama for at least one year before the first day of classes. Each university has specific residency requirements, so refer to your university's residency guidelines for detailed information.
Q. What support services are available for international students?

A. International students have access to a range of services including orientation sessions, English language support, visa and immigration assistance, and cultural integration activities. The Office of International Student Services at each college can provide more detailed support and information.
Q. How do I choose a major?


A. Choosing a major involves reflecting on your interests, strengths, and career goals. Utilize career counseling services at your school to explore different fields. Consider internships or general education courses to gain exposure to various disciplines. Most colleges allow you to declare or change your major by the end of your sophomore year.

Q. Can out-of-state students receive financial aid in Alabama?

A. Yes, out-of-state students are eligible for many types of financial aid including federal loans, grants, and many scholarships offered by the institutions and external entities. However, state-funded scholarships and grants may have residency requirements. Always check the specific eligibility criteria for each type of aid.
Q. How do I manage my health care needs while at college?

A. Colleges provide health services that include primary care, mental health services, emergency medical care, and sometimes pharmacy services. Students are typically required to have health insurance, which can be procured through the university or an external provider. Health centers on campus offer convenient access to care and more information can be found on their respective websites.
Q. Are there programs for students with disabilities?

A. Yes, colleges in Alabama are equipped with Offices of Disability Services that provide accommodations, support services, and resources to ensure that students with disabilities can access and participate in educational opportunities. Services include note-taking assistance, extended test time, interpretation services, and technology aids. Contact the collegeâ€™s disability services office for specific support options.
Q. What transportation options are available for students?

A. Many colleges offer campus shuttle services to assist with transportation around campus and sometimes local areas. For students without cars, services like bikes, scooters, and public transit are available. Additionally, some schools may partner with ride-sharing services to offer safe and affordable transportation options.
Q. How can parents be involved in the college experience?

A. Many colleges offer parent-specific orientations, family weekends, and newsletters that keep parents informed about campus events and important academic dates. Parents can also participate in Parent Associations, which offer opportunities to engage with the campus community and administration.
